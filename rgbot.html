RGBot

Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 15, 2026  ·  Developer: Miguel P. Pais / Clarityscape

RGBot does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. No account is required to use the app.

What this app does

RGBot is a real-time color identification assistant. It uses your device's camera to analyze colors in your surroundings and identify them by name, along with their RGB, HEX, CMYK, Pantone, and RAL values. You can save colors to a personal collection and share or copy them at any time. All image processing happens entirely on your device.

Data we collect

None. RGBot works entirely offline. No analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising SDKs, and no third-party services are included in the app.

Device permissions

  • Camera — used to capture a live feed for real-time color identification. No images or video are ever recorded, stored, or transmitted outside your device.
  • Flashlight / Torch — used optionally to illuminate dark environments for more accurate color readings. It is only activated when you explicitly toggle it on.

Cloud backup

Your saved colors, language preference, and app settings are stored locally on your device. If you have cloud backup enabled on your device, your operating system may back up this data as part of its normal app backup process:

  • Android: Google may back up app data to your Google Drive account via Android Auto Backup, subject to your Google account settings.
  • iOS: Apple may sync app settings to your iCloud account via iCloud Key-Value Storage, subject to your iCloud settings.

This backup is handled entirely by your device's operating system and your own cloud account. Clarityscape has no access to this data.

Children's privacy

RGBot does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new effective date. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
